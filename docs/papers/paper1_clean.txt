PM10 and PM2.5 are of significant concern due to their adverse health effects. PM10, commonly called inhalable particulate matter, can penetrate the nasal and oral cavities, while PM2.5, known as respirable particulate matter, can reach the bronchioles and alveoli. With a smaller diameter than PM10, PM2.5 remains suspended in the atmosphere for extended peri- ods and can travel longer distances. Consequently, it acts as a critical carrier of toxic and harmful substances, exertin a more profound and widespread impact on human health and air quality ( Zhu et al. , 2021 ). As a key factor affectin urban air quality, PM2.5 is routinely mon- itored across multiple sites to provide data on its concentration. These data support research into pollution sources and spatial distribution, enablin a better understandin of urban air quality and formulatin effective air pollution control strategies ( Hedayatzadeh et al. , 2024 ). However, spatiotemporal monitorin data often suffer from missin records due to equipment malfunctions, calibration processes, mainte- nance, communication issues, or other technical difficulties ( Zhu et al. , 2024 ). Such missin values disrupt the coherence of analyses, compro- misin the accurate assessment of pollution trends and characteristics. This, in turn, delays the formulation and implementation of targeted mitigation measures. This issue becomes particularly concernin when a large proportion of the data is missing, as it may introduce significant bias in subsequent spa- tiotemporal analyses and decision-makin processes related to pollution control ( Zhan and Thorburn , 2022 ). Therefore, accurately imputin these missin records is essential for thoroughly evaluatin pollu- tion's environmental impacts and developin sustainable air quality management strategies ( Zhao et al. , 2025 ). Most existin imputation methods assume specific missin mecha- nisms in their development. The underlyin missin data mechanism significantly influences the reliability and effectiveness of imputation techniques ( Kumar and Varija , 2024 ). Rubin ( 1976 ) initially described three types of missin data mechanisms. Missin completely at ran- dom (MCAR) occurs when the probability of missin data is entirely unrelated to any factors, with each missin value arisin purely by chance. Missin at random (MAR) arises when the probability of missin data is unrelated to the value of the missin data itself but depends on other observed factors. For example, durin peak electricity usage, monitorin sites may experience missin records due to insuffi- cient power supply. Missin not at random (MNAR) occurs when the probability of missin data depends on the value of the missin data itself, or potentially on external factors, as seen when excessively high PM2.5 concentrations cause particle accumulation on sensor surfaces, impairin the performance of detection components. Beyond these mechanisms, two other missin data patterns frequently encountered in real-world applications are Missin in Sequence (MIS) and Missin in Block (MIB) ( Du et al. , 2024 ). For instance, in the spatiotemporal data of PM2.5 monitorin sites, MIS refers to continuous missin data at a single site over a specific period, while MIB describes situations where multiple sites experience continuous missin data simultaneously over the same period. These patterns, characterized by their continuity and spatial correlation, pose considerable challenges for data recovery and subsequent analysis. Traditional methods for missin values imputation, such as Multiple Imputation by Chained Equations (MICE), K-Nearest Neighbors Impu- tation (KNNI), matrix factorization (MF), and iterative singular value decomposition (iterative SVD), have been widely adopted due to their solid theoretical foundations and ease of implementation ( Van Buuren and Oudshoorn , 2000 ; Cover and Hart , 1967 ; Koren et al. , 2009 ; Troyanskaya et al. , 2001 ). However, these approaches may struggle to capture complex non-linear relationships in real-world datasets. Recent studies have extensively explored missin values imputa- tion to address various missingness mechanisms and feature char- acteristics, evolvin from traditional statistical methods to more re- cent deep learning-based models, such as Generative Adversarial Net- works (GANs) ( Goodfellow et al. , 2014 ). Miao et al. ( 2021 ) pro- posed a novel semi-supervised GAN (SSGAN), which improves im- putation performance for multivariate time series data by integrat- in a semi-supervised classifier and a temporal reminder matrix. Du et al. ( 2023 ) proposed Self-Attention-based Imputation for Time Series (SAITS) for estimatin missin values in multivariate time series, which captures temporal dependencies and feature correlations through two diagonally-masked self-attention (DMSA) blocks. Yuan et al. ( 2024 ) introduced a stacked adaptive median iterative imputation framework (SAMIIF) for missin data recovery in time series. The framework incor- porates movin window and dynamic sample overlay techniques, with an adaptive learnin mechanism, to iteratively refine the imputation process. Yuan et al. ( 2023 ) proposed an attention-based interval-aided network (AIA-Net) to impute missin values in heterogeneous time series. This method integrates attention-based time-aware dynamic im- putation with an interval-aided time-aware module to model temporal dependencies adaptively. SSGAN, SAITS, and SAMIIF are primarily limited to MCAR-type missin data and may degrade significantly in real-world settings where such assumptions rarely hold. At the same time, AIA-Net is not well-suited for the MIB pattern. Hu et al. ( 2020 ) proposed a trinetwork-based GAN (tnGAN), em- ployin scope-aware feature extraction and information-aware feature integration to capture temporal evolution and spatial similarity better. This method may be less effective in handlin high missin rates, as evaluation was limited to moderate missin conditions (not exceedin 33.33%). Hu et al. ( 2021 ) proposed a hierarchical GAN-based data recovery method designed to handle varyin numbers of incomplete data. A joint attention module was introduced to extract spatiotem- poral dependencies and integrate cross-layer features. However, this proposed method requires complete data pairs for training, which may not always be available in practice. Liu et al. ( 2023 ) proposed Inverted Transformers (iTransformer), which applies attention and feed-forward networks to inverted dimensions. It embeds time points into variable tokens to better capture multivariate correlations and non-linear rep- resentations. Nonetheless, its performance advantages may be limited in scenarios with no external feature variables ( Zhan and Thorburn , 2022 ; Radii et al. , 2023 ). Che et al. ( 2018 ) proposed the Gated Recurrent Unit with trainable Decays (GRUD), which incorporates a decay mechanism to model the impact of missin values over time. Ou et al. ( 2024 ) proposed a Position-Encodin Denoisin Autoencoder (PE-DAE) for imputin missin data in time series. This method leverages temporal autocor- relation and the positions of missin values, integratin a self-paced learnin trainin strategy to enhance performance. Although effective in practice, GRUD and PE-DAE involve model components that must be carefully adapted to the characteristics of the target data, makin them less flexible across diverse applications. For instance, GRUD requires domain-specific design of its decay mechanism, while PE-DAE involves tunin parameters for the self-paced learnin strategy, which can be challengin in practice. In this study, we propose a Varying-Coefficient Autoregressive Ad- versarial Network (VCAAN) framework for the imputation of missin values in spatiotemporal data. This framework first utilizes a Varying- Coefficient Autoregressive model (VCA) to thoroughly incorporate the correlations between missin data and historical, future, and surround- in information. By capturin the dynamic dependencies inherent in spatiotemporal data, VCA also requires estimatin fewer parameters than Vector Autoregressive (VAR) ( Sims , 1980 ). Subsequently, we de- sign a Convolutional Discriminative Network (CDN) to assess the im- puted missin values, trainin it adversarially alongside the VCA. To further enhance robustness, we introduce a dynamic adversarial loss weightin mechanism, which mitigates overfittin to a certain extent durin trainin (inspired by Du et al. , 2023 ; Huan et al. , 2024 ). Notably, the proposed method enables joint trainin and imputation on incomplete datasets, eliminatin the need for separate trainin and testin phases. It operates without requirin external feature variables and offers a versatile framework that can be applied to a wide range of imputation tasks. Finally, a series of experiments usin the PM2.5 concentration data in Beijin is conducted to validate the effectiveness of the proposed method. In particular, the main contributions of this study are as follows. (1) A novel hybrid method is proposed that integrates statistical methods with deep learnin into a unified framework. This method provides enhanced robustness and flexibility, enablin effective imputation across diverse real-world missin data types and high missin rate scenarios. (2) A VCA module is introduced to jointly capture the relationships between missin values and their temporal-spatial context, in- cludin historical, future, and adjacent locations. The module also effectively models the intrinsic dynamic dependencies in spatiotemporal data, all within a simplified model structure. (3) A CDN module is specifically designed for spatiotemporal im- putation and integrated with VCA through adversarial training. Furthermore, a dynamic adversarial loss weightin mechanism enhances adaptability in challengin scenarios. (4) Extensive experiments are performed to evaluate the performance of the proposed method and competitive methods. The proposed method shows eneral advantages under various scenarios. L. Xian and H. 4. Discussion We proposed a novel hybrid method that combines statistical meth- ods and deep learnin to improve the imputation of PM2.5 spatiotem- poral data in complex terrains and densely populated urban areas, addressin various missingness patterns and rates. Although the VAR model is a classic approach for multivariate time series analysis, it is not directly applicable due to the diversity and complexity of missin data scenarios in spatiotemporal settings ( Sims , 1980 ; Bashir and Wei , 2018 ). Meanwhile, GANs, as a powerful deep enerative modelin tech- nique, face challenges in effectively capturin spatiotemporal patterns in missin values ( Brimos et al. , 2024 ; Wan et al. , 2022 ). Integrat- in both approaches' strengths can largely improve the accuracy of imputation of the missin data. The proposed method incorporates autoregression to capture the spatiotemporal dependencies of missin values, usin B-spline functions to represent the dynamic coefficients. Inspired by GANs, we design a Convolutional Discriminator Network to distinguish between real and imputed values in the spatiotemporal data. Dynamic adversarial loss weight is introduced in the adversarial trainin process to adap- tively adjust the weights of the enerator and discriminator, effectively preventin overfitting. Traditional imputation models typically require splittin the dataset into trainin and testin sets, which has two main drawbacks: first, it limits data utilization, especially when data is scarce, potentially impactin model performance; Second, for spatiotemporal data, the simple temporal or spatial split may lead to overfittin due to the complex dependencies between time and space dimensions. In con- trast, the proposed model is trained directly on the observed data without splitting, fully utilizin all available information and avoidin information loss. By learnin the overall distribution of spatiotemporal data directly from the observed data, the model can better capture spatiotemporal dependencies and achieve more accurate imputation. This approach simplifies the trainin process and significantly improves the model's robustness and practicality, makin it particularly suitable for spatiotemporal data imputation tasks. Extensive experiments demonstrate the remarkable potential of the proposed method. To validate effectiveness of our model, five missin rates and five missingness patterns are considered in the experiments. Our model outperformed other methods under low, medium, and high missin rates (except for MNAR-low). For MCAR and MAR, our model is not sensitive to the initial imputation values and converges stably. However, under MNAR with medium and high missin rates, the model's performance is kind of sensitive to the initial values mean- in that the model needs further improvement. For MIS and MIB, the former disrupts long-term temporal dependencies, while the latter preserves some local spatiotemporal information, allowin for more accurate imputation. Our model is less stable in the former scenario but significantly reduces imputation loss (up to 81.88% in the upper quartile and 38.93% in the median). The proposed method is crucial for repairin incomplete PM2.5 spatiotemporal data, subsequent exposure assessment, and health im- pact studies. Besides its effectiveness in the current application, the proposed method is highly versatile. It can be readily extended to other spatiotemporal pollutant data such as NO2, SO2, and O3, as well as to other types of correlated multivariate time series. We recommend applyin the method to offline spatiotemporal datasets with no more than 20 spatial monitorin stations and samplin intervals higher than 15 min (e.g., 15 min to 1 h per observation), under which our experi- mental results demonstrated stable and reliable performance. Despite the promisin results, there is room for improvement in handlin spatiotemporal data of large-scale sites and high missin rates under the MNAR mechanism. In the future, we will incorporate additional contex- tual information (such as meteorological data and sensor status) and re- duce the model's reliance on pre-estimation algorithms, aimin to bet- ter handle spatiotemporal data of large-scale sites and MNAR scenarios.
